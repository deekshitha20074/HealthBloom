Blackheads and Whiteheads: Quick Guide

What are they?

Blackheads and whiteheads are types of acne caused by clogged pores, with blackheads appearing open and dark, while whiteheads remain closed and white.

Common Causes

  • Excess oil production
  • Dead skin cells clogging pores
  • Hormonal changes or imbalances
  • Using oily or comedogenic skincare products

Quick Tips to Prevent and Treat

  1. Cleanse Regularly: Use a gentle cleanser to remove excess oil and dirt without irritating the skin.
  2. Exfoliate Gently: Use an exfoliator 1-2 times a week to remove dead skin cells and prevent clogged pores.
  3. Avoid Touching Your Face: Keep hands off your face to prevent transferring bacteria and oil.
  4. Use Non-Comedogenic Products: Choose skincare products labeled as non-comedogenic to avoid clogging pores.
  5. Apply Topical Treatments: Use salicylic acid or benzoyl peroxide treatments to reduce blackheads and whiteheads.

When to Seek Help

If over-the-counter treatments don't help, or the condition worsens, consult a dermatologist for more effective options.
